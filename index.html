Who May Qualify

The Radiation Exposure Compensation Act (RECA) covers several categories of people exposed to radiation from nuclear testing and uranium mining.

Downwinders

People who lived in designated Utah, Nevada, or Arizona counties during above-ground nuclear tests from 1951–1962 and later developed a covered illness.

14,092 pending downwinder claims

Uranium Workers

Miners, millers, and ore transporters who worked in the uranium industry and later developed a covered condition like lung cancer or pulmonary fibrosis.

Uranium mining states covered

Onsite Participants

Military personnel, civilian employees, and contractors who were present at atmospheric nuclear tests or participated in post-test operations.

Active duty & civilian covered

Survivors & Families

Surviving spouses, children, and other relatives of deceased individuals who would have qualified under RECA may be eligible to file on their behalf.

Survivor claims accepted

RECA Claim Categories

PL 119-21 and legacy RECA legislation cover multiple exposure types. Find yours below.

Downwinder Claims

For individuals who resided in specific counties in Utah, Nevada, and Arizona during atmospheric nuclear testing (1951–1962) and developed a covered illness such as leukemia, thyroid cancer, or lung cancer.

16,492 downwinder claims filed  |  14,092 pending
DOJ June 2026

Uranium Worker Claims

Miners, millers, and ore transporters who worked with uranium and developed covered diseases including lung cancer, pulmonary fibrosis, renal cancer, and other conditions listed under RECA statute.

Multiple states covered  |  Exposure 1942–1971+

Onsite Participant Claims

Military and civilian personnel present at atmospheric nuclear tests, underground tests (post-1962), or post-test operations including Trinity, Operation Crossroads, and Nevada Test Site programs.

Active duty, reserves, & civilian contractors

Legacy Claims (Pre-July 2025)

Claims filed under the legacy RECA statute before the July 3, 2025 reauthorization deadline. PL 119-21 may provide a new path for previously denied claims.

57,081 legacy filed  |  42,596 approved
DOJ June 2026

Survivor & Family Claims

Surviving spouses, children, and parents of deceased individuals who would have qualified under RECA may file claims on behalf of their loved ones.

Survivor relationship proof required

Manhattan Project Claims

Workers and community members near Manhattan Project waste sites may qualify under expanded PL 119-21 geographic criteria.
